Mexican Chorizo

Description

Chorizo is a type of pork sausage originating from the Iberian Peninsula. They get their characteristic red color from dried, smoked, red peppers. It can serve as a replacement for ground beef or pork.

Mexican Chorizo are made not just from fatty pork, but also from beef, venison, chicken, and turkey. The meat is usually ground and different seasonings are used than the chorizo found in Europe or Asia.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp ground chile
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp dried Mexican oregano
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 3/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds, crush
  • 3/4 tsp white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 lb. ground pork, chilled

Steps

  1. Combine chile powder, vinegar, oregano, water, garlic, salt, red pepper flakes, cumin, seed, sugar, and pepper in a small bowl.
    • Mix until the salt and sugar are fully dissolved.
  2. Break up the pork in a separate bowl.
  3. Add spice mixture to the pork and knead thoroughly.
    • Use immediately or refrigerate 8-12 hours to let flavors meld.
  4. Cook and stir on a skillet over medium heat for about 10 minutes until browned and crumbly.
